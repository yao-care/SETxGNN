## Cytotoxicity

Cisplatin is a well-established conventional cytotoxic chemotherapy agent. The following profile is based on widely published pharmacological and clinical data:

| Item | Content |
|------|------|
| Cytotoxicity Classification | Conventional cytotoxic (Platinum-based alkylating-like agent) |
| Myelosuppression Risk | **High** — dose-limiting myelosuppression; leukopenia, thrombocytopenia, and anaemia are common |
| Emetogenicity Classification | **High** (one of the most emetogenic chemotherapy agents; requires triple antiemetic prophylaxis) |
| Monitoring Items | CBC with differential, serum creatinine/BUN, creatinine clearance, electrolytes (Mg²⁺, K⁺, Ca²⁺, Na⁺), liver function tests, audiometry, neurological assessment |
| Handling Protection | **Must follow cytotoxic drug handling regulations** — closed-system transfer devices recommended; PPE (double gloving, gown, eye protection) required during preparation and administration |

**Additional nephrotoxicity note:** Cisplatin is well-known for dose-dependent nephrotoxicity. Aggressive intravenous hydration and monitoring of renal function are mandatory. Cumulative ototoxicity and peripheral neuropathy are also significant long-term concerns.
