## Why is This Prediction Reasonable?

Currently, detailed mechanism of action data is not available in this evidence pack. Based on widely known pharmacological information, Celecoxib is a selective cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) inhibitor belonging to the non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) class. It works by selectively blocking the COX-2 enzyme, thereby reducing the production of prostaglandins involved in inflammation, pain, and fever. Internationally, it is marketed under the brand name **Celebrex** and is approved for conditions including osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, acute pain, and primary dysmenorrhea.

Celecoxib has also attracted considerable research interest in oncology, particularly for familial adenomatous polyposis (FAP) and as a chemopreventive agent in colorectal cancer, owing to the role of COX-2 overexpression in tumourigenesis.
